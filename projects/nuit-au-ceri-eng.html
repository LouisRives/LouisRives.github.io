A Night at CERI

◆  HORROR  ·  FIRST-PERSON  ·  UNITY  ◆

✦

Developed as part of the "24 Hours to Code" event organized by the University of Avignon, A Night at CERI is a first-person horror game created with Unity by a team of three collaborators and myself. The main challenge of this event was to divide tasks effectively within a very limited timeframe.

The concept is simple: the player finds themselves trapped inside the CERI (Center for Computer Science Education and Research) and must escape by collecting three hidden keys while avoiding the monster roaming the corridors.

This experience marked my first time using Unity, which I discovered and learned throughout the project. My primary responsibilities were managing the game's graphical aspect. I started by remodelling the CERI, recreating its structures and ensuring a faithful representation of the real environment. I also designed and placed objects within the scene, carefully arranging them to balance immersion and playability. A significant part of my work involved adjusting lighting and visual effects, including implementing a functional flashlight to enhance the game's atmosphere. Although I created several elements myself, some assets were sourced from the Unity Asset Store to save time and ensure graphical consistency. This first experience with Unity allowed me to develop my skills and contribute to the project creatively.

Main features:

  • The game map is a full 3D model of the CERI building
  • The AI can spot the player and initiate a chase, which ends if the player successfully evades it
  • A pathfinding system allows the AI to pursue the player effectively
  • When the AI has not detected the player, it freely patrols the map
  • Key spawn locations are randomised each run
