A Night at CERI

Developed as part of the "24 hours to Code" event organized by the University of Avignon, "A Night at CERI" is a first-person horror game created with Unity by a team of three collaborators and myself. The main challenge of this event was to divide tasks within a limited timeframe.

The game’s concept is simple: the player finds themselves trapped in the CERI (Center for Computer Science Education and Research) and must escape by collecting three hidden keys while avoiding the roaming monster.

This experience marked my first time using Unity, which I discovered and learned throughout the project. My primary responsibilities were managing the game’s graphical aspect. I started by remodeling the CERI, recreating its structures, and ensuring a faithful representation of the environment. I also designed and placed objects within the environment, thoughtfully arranging them to balance immersion and playability. A significant part of my work involved adjusting lighting and visual effects, including implementing a functional flashlight to enhance the game’s atmosphere. Lastly, although I created several elements myself, some assets were sourced from the Unity Store to save time and ensure graphical consistency. This first experience with Unity allowed me to develop my skills and contribute to the project creatively.

Main features:

  • The game map is a 3D model of the CERI
  • The AI can spot the player and initiate a chase, which ends if the player successfully evades it
  • A pathfinding system is used by the AI to effectively pursue the player
  • If the AI has not detected the player, it freely patrols the map
  • The keys’ locations are randomized
